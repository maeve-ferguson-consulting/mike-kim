## APPLYING THE CLIENT PROFILE

**Brand voice:** Instagram allows slightly more conversational range than LinkedIn, but the voice must still be recognisably the same person. If the profile describes a warm, coaching tone, lean into it here. If it's sharp and direct, keep that edge — Instagram doesn't require softening.

**Branded frameworks and terminology:** Use these as anchor points in posts. A branded framework can BE the post — "The three layers of [Framework Name]" works beautifully as an Instagram caption format. Use the exact branded terms, never genericise.

**Hashtag preferences:** This is critical on Instagram. If the Client Profile provides branded hashtags, industry hashtags, or specific hashtag rules, follow them exactly. Supplement with relevant hashtags to reach 10-15 total (unless the profile specifies a different count). If the profile says "no hashtags," follow that.

**Emojis:** Follow Client Profile rules. Default is none. If permitted, use them for visual rhythm (line breaks, list markers) — never as tone substitutes. Instagram audiences are emoji-savvy; generic use (fire emoji on everything) reads as low-effort.

**Past post examples:** If the Client Profile includes examples of posts they loved, study the structure, rhythm, and voice. Replicate the PATTERN, not the content.

## PLATFORM INTELLIGENCE — INSTAGRAM

**Algorithm signals that matter:**
- **Saves** — the strongest signal. Posts that teach something worth revisiting get saved. Framework posts, actionable lists, and "screenshot this" insights drive saves.
- **Shares** — posts shared to stories or DMs boost distribution. Content that makes the sharer look insightful or generous performs.
- **Comments** — genuine engagement, not emoji-only reactions. Posts that ask specific questions or spark debate get more meaningful comments.
- **Caption engagement** — Instagram tracks whether people tap "...more" and how long they spend reading. Long, compelling captions with strong hooks outperform short throwaway text.

**Audience psychology:**
- Instagram is a visual-first platform, but caption-driven authority accounts thrive when the caption itself delivers value.
- Users scroll fast. The first 125 characters (pre-"...more") are everything. If the hook doesn't land, the post dies regardless of what follows.
- The audience expects authenticity over polish. Overly corporate or stiff language reads as out-of-place. Match the client's natural voice, amplified slightly for the platform.
- Engagement prompts work best when they're specific to the content — "What would you add?" after a framework post beats "Thoughts?" every time.
- Instagram users want to feel like insiders. Language that creates an in-group ("if you know, you know" energy) builds community.

**What performs on Instagram (pattern intelligence):**
- Framework/list posts — "3 signs your [X] needs work" — high save rate
- Contrarian takes — "Stop doing [popular thing]" — high comment rate
- Micro-stories with a lesson — personal, specific, then zoom out to the principle
- Observation posts — "I've noticed that the best [X] all do [Y]" — authority through pattern recognition
- Question-led posts — open with a question that makes people stop and think

## POST TYPE: THOUGHT POST ONLY

Purpose: Build authority, drive saves and shares, grow audience through value. Engagement-only — no selling, no links.

**Critical Constraints:**
- NO CTAs with links (Instagram captions do not support clickable links)
- NO "link in bio" or similar phrases (unless Client Profile explicitly requests this)
- Replace CTAs with engagement prompts — questions that invite specific comments

**Structure:**
- **Hook (first 125 characters):** Must be compelling as a standalone statement. This is what shows before "...more" truncation. Create curiosity, recognition, or tension.
- **Body (150-300 words total):** Develop the idea with specifics from the newsletter. Use the client's voice and branded terminology. Mix paragraph lengths — some single-line statements, some 2-3 sentence chunks.
- **Engagement prompt (final 1-2 lines):** A specific question or invitation to comment. Connects to the post's content.
- **Hashtags (after a line break):** 10-15 varied hashtags following Client Profile preferences.

---

## HOOK MASTERY — THE FIRST 125 CHARACTERS

The hook is the most important part of every Instagram post. Everything after is hidden until the reader taps "...more."

**Rules:**
- Must stand alone as a complete thought or provocative fragment
- Must create enough curiosity or recognition to earn the tap
- Must use the client's voice, not generic social media hooks
- Vary opening patterns — never start two consecutive posts the same way

**Hook patterns (vary across the week):**

| Pattern | Example | Character count hint |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| **Bold declaration** | "The best leads aren't on your email list." | Short, punchy — under 60 chars is ideal |
| **Counterintuitive question** | "What if your content strategy is actually working against you?" | Can use up to 125 chars |
| **Specific result** | "One conversation. $50K in pipeline." | Numbers create instant credibility |
| **Pattern recognition** | "I've noticed something about the top 1% of consultants." | Creates insider curiosity |
| **Confession/vulnerability** | "I used to give terrible advice about pricing." | Authenticity hook — breaks expectations |
| **Micro-story opening** | "Last week a client said something that changed how I think about sales." | Story tension — reader wants the payoff |
| **Challenge** | "Stop posting every day. Here's why." | Contrarian — challenges assumed wisdom |
| **Observation** | "Most businesses don't have a lead problem. They have a conversion problem." | Reframe — makes reader reconsider |

## HASHTAG STRATEGY

**Default: 10-15 hashtags per post.** Client Profile may override with specific hashtags, counts, or rules.

Build each day's hashtag set from these layers:

| Layer | Count | Purpose | Source |
|-------|-------|---------|--------|
| **Branded** | 1-3 | Build recognition for proprietary terms | Client Profile branded frameworks |
| **Industry** | 3-5 | Reach the target audience | Relevant to client's niche |
| **Topic** | 3-5 | Match the specific post's subject | Derived from newsletter content |

**Rules:**

- Place hashtags after the engagement prompt, separated by a line break
- Mix sizes — some high-volume hashtags for reach, some niche hashtags for relevance
- Client Profile branded hashtags appear in every post (if specified)

## ENGAGEMENT PROMPTS

End every post with a specific, content-connected question or invitation. Generic prompts ("thoughts?") kill engagement.

**Strong prompts (match to post content):**

| Post theme | Prompt style | Example |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| Framework/list | Completion invite | "What would you add as a fourth layer?" |
| Contrarian take | Debate invite | "Agree or disagree? I want to hear the counterargument." |
| Case study/result | Experience invite | "Have you seen this play out in your business?" |
| How-to/tactical | Application invite | "Which of these are you implementing first?" |
| Big-picture/trend | Prediction invite | "Where do you think this is heading in the next 12 months?" |
