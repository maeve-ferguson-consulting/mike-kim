# The Beacon Pattern — Newsletter Structure

The Beacon pattern is the structural framework for every newsletter. The newsletter is a lighthouse — it illuminates one truth clearly enough that the right reader sees themselves in it and moves toward the light.

## Structure

### 1. The Hook (Opening)
A specific moment. Not a concept, not a question, not a quote — a moment. Something that happened. Drop the reader into the middle of it.

- "A client said something on Tuesday that stopped me cold."
- "I was standing in the school pickup line when it hit me."
- "Three minutes into the call, she started crying."

The hook earns the next paragraph. That's its only job.

### 2. The Expansion (The Insight)
Zoom out from the moment. What does it mean? What pattern does it reveal? Connect the specific to the universal — but stay grounded in the story. Don't leap to abstractions.

This is where the client's IP, frameworks, and expertise come in. The moment becomes a lens for a bigger truth.

### 3. The Mirror (The Reader Connection)
Turn the insight toward the reader. "You've felt this too." "This is what's happening when you..." "If you're the kind of person who..."

The reader should see themselves in the story. Not because it's generic — because it's specific enough to resonate with the right person and bounce off the wrong one.

### 4. The Beacon (The Offer Tether)
Land it. This is not a hard sell. It's a natural extension: "If this resonates, here's where to go deeper." The offer is the logical next step for someone who just had a realization.

- Subtle: Mention the framework or methodology by name in the context of the story
- Direct: "This is exactly what we work on inside [Offer Name]"
- Invitation: "If you're ready to [outcome], [CTA with link]"

Match the intensity to the story. Heavy emotional piece = subtle tether. Action-oriented piece = direct invitation.

## Calibration Notes

- **Length:** Match the client's typical newsletter. Some write 500 words, some write 2,000. Don't change their length — that's part of their voice.
- **Frequency of offer tether:** Every newsletter, but the intensity varies. Not every issue is a sales pitch. Most are trust deposits.
- **Images/formatting:** Follow the client's existing style. Some use headers, some write in one continuous flow. Match, don't impose.

## Working With a Newsletter Brief

When a Newsletter Brief is provided (from the Content Planner), the Beacon pattern fields are pre-mapped:

- **The Hook** draws from the Brief's **Story Entry Point** and the **Belief Shift** wrong belief
- **The Expansion** draws from the Brief's **Blind Spot** (the reveal) and uses the framework element
- **The Beacon** draws from the Brief's **Expanded Vision** (the right belief) and **Offer Connection**

The brief provides the strategic architecture. Your job is to bring it to life with the client's raw weekly material, voice, and specific stories.
