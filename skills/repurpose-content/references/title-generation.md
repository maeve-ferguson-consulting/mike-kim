# Title Generation — Phase 3 Methodology

Generate 4 distinct newsletter title options, each using a different engagement strategy. These titles are for the newsletter itself (not social posts) and should be optimized for the platforms where the newsletter is distributed (from Project Knowledge — e.g., Substack, LinkedIn Newsletter, email).

---

## Title Strategies

Generate one title per strategy:

### 1. Data-Driven

Uses numbers, statistics, or specific metrics from the newsletter. Specificity signals credibility and promises concrete value.

**Pattern:** Lead with the number or metric. Make the outcome tangible.

**Examples of the pattern (do not copy — generate from the actual newsletter):**
- "The 3-Layer Content Strategy That Builds $50K Pipelines"
- "Why 73% of Consultants Undercharge (And the Fix Takes 15 Minutes)"

### 2. Question-Based

Poses a provocative question that demands an answer. The question should challenge an assumption the reader holds or identify a gap they haven't noticed.

**Pattern:** Ask something the reader cannot ignore. The answer must be in the newsletter.

**Examples of the pattern:**
- "What If Your Best Clients Are Avoiding Your Content?"
- "Are You Building Authority or Just Making Noise?"

### 3. How-To / Framework

Promises a system, method, or actionable guide. Positions the newsletter as a blueprint, not just commentary.

**Pattern:** Promise a specific outcome through a specific mechanism.

**Examples of the pattern:**
- "How to Turn One Conversation Into a Year of Content"
- "The Expert's Playbook for Premium Positioning"

### 4. Contrarian / Bold

Challenges conventional wisdom or makes a strong claim. Should feel intellectually stimulating, not clickbait.

**Pattern:** State the opposite of what the audience expects. Back it up in the newsletter.

**Examples of the pattern:**
- "Stop Creating Content. Start Building Infrastructure."
- "The Worst Marketing Advice Coaches Keep Repeating"

---

## Per-Title Output

For each of the 4 options:

```
### Option [N]: [Title]

**Strategy:** [Data-Driven / Question-Based / How-To / Contrarian]
**Character Count:** [XX characters]
**Why This Works:** [2-3 sentences explaining:
  - What engagement hook is at work (curiosity gap, specificity, urgency, authority signal, transformation promise)
  - Why it fits the target audience (from Project Knowledge)
  - How it connects to the newsletter's core theme]
```

---

## Quality Standards

**Each title must:**
- Be 50-70 characters (flag if longer and explain why)
- Front-load important keywords
- Work across the client's distribution platforms
- Feel sophisticated, not clickbait
- Reflect actual newsletter content — no bait-and-switch
- Use a distinct strategy from the other 3 options

**Avoid:**
- Generic titles that could apply to any newsletter
- Jargon the target audience would not use
- Overly long titles (over 80 characters) without justification
- Titles that promise content not in the newsletter
- Emojis in titles

---

## SEO Considerations

- Include primary keyword from the newsletter topic naturally
- Use power words that drive clicks when they fit genuinely ("proven," "essential," "hidden")
- Front-load the most important words — many platforms truncate after 60-70 characters
- Consider how the title displays in both email subject lines and platform feeds
