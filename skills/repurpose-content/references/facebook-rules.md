## APPLYING THE CLIENT PROFILE

**Brand voice:** Facebook is the warmest platform in this suite. The voice here should be the same person as LinkedIn and Instagram, but in their most relatable, human mode. If the Client Profile describes a direct, authoritative voice, the Facebook version still has that substance — but the delivery is more like a conversation with someone you respect, not a stage presentation.

**Branded frameworks and terminology:** Use naturally, but embed them in stories and conversations rather than presenting them formally. On LinkedIn you might say "The Authority Flywheel has three stages." On Facebook you'd say "I was thinking about why some people build authority effortlessly while others grind — and it comes back to the Flywheel."

**Language to avoid:** Apply all Client Profile restrictions. Facebook's informal tone doesn't override brand constraints.

**Hashtags:** None by default on Facebook. If the Client Profile overrides this, follow their rules. Facebook hashtags have low discovery value compared to Instagram — most brands skip them.

**Emojis:** Follow Client Profile rules. Default is none. If permitted, Facebook tolerates more emoji use than LinkedIn — they can add warmth and visual breaks in a community context.

**Discussion prompts:** If the Client Profile has preferences for engagement style (e.g., "always end with a question," "prefer 'what do you think?' over direct debate"), honour those.

## PLATFORM INTELLIGENCE — FACEBOOK

**Algorithm signals that matter:**
- **Meaningful interactions** — Facebook explicitly prioritises posts that generate "meaningful interactions between people." Comments, especially back-and-forth conversations, are the strongest signal.
- **Comment length and quality** — longer, more substantive comments signal meaningful interaction. Posts that provoke thoughtful responses (not just emoji reactions) get wider distribution.
- **Shares** — content shared to feeds or groups extends reach significantly. Content that's useful, surprising, or makes the sharer look thoughtful gets shared.
- **Video and native content** — links to external sites get suppressed. That's why CTA posts use "Link in first comment."
- **Group amplification** — if the client's audience is active in Facebook groups, content that resonates can be shared into groups for exponential reach.

**Audience psychology:**
- Facebook users are in community mode, not professional mode. They're scrolling between family photos, news, and group discussions. Your content needs to feel like it belongs in that stream — not like a LinkedIn post that wandered into the wrong party.
- The ~480 character "See More" threshold is your hook window. Everything before that fold must create enough pull to earn the click. On mobile, this is even shorter.
- Facebook audiences respond to vulnerability and relatability more than any other platform. The client can be an authority AND a human. "I'm still figuring this out, but here's what I've learned" lands harder here than LinkedIn's "Here's the definitive framework."
- People share content that reflects their identity. Posts that make the sharer feel smart, generous, or part of a community get shared.
- Discussion prompts that feel genuine (not engagement-bait) drive the best comments. "What's your experience?" beats "Drop a [emoji] if you agree."

**What performs on Facebook (pattern intelligence):**
- Story-driven posts — personal experiences with professional insights woven in
- "Can we talk about..." openings — creates community feel, invites participation
- Hot takes on industry norms — "Am I the only one who thinks...?"
- Helpful frameworks presented conversationally — "Something I've been doing with clients lately..."
- Question-first posts — open with a genuine question, then share your perspective

## POST TYPES

### Thought Post (1 per day)

Purpose: Spark genuine discussion, build community, demonstrate expertise through the client's most human voice. No selling.

**Structure:**
- **Hook (within ~480 characters):** Must land before the "See More" fold. Create curiosity, recognition, or an emotional response. The first 1-2 sentences are critical — they're visible in the feed preview.
- **Body (3-6 short paragraphs):** Develop the idea conversationally. Use the client's voice at its warmest. Share perspectives, not proclamations. Use "I've noticed..." and "What I've been thinking about..." and "Here's what I keep seeing..." framings.
- **Discussion prompt (final 1-2 lines):** A genuine question that invites specific responses. The best prompts make people want to share their own experience.

### CTA Post (1 per day)

Purpose: Drive action toward the weekly offer/CTA. Should feel like a natural extension of the community conversation, not a sales post interrupting it.

**Structure:**
- **Hook (within ~480 characters):** Connects to a real challenge or aspiration the audience has. Conversational, not sales-y.
- **Build (3-5 short paragraphs):** Use the client's warm, relatable tone. Create desire through empathy — "If you've been feeling [pain]..." or "For those of you who are ready to..."
- **Bridge (1-2 lines):** Natural transition that makes the CTA feel like a gift, not a pitch.
- **CTA (exact text from input):** The weekly CTA as provided.
- **Final line:** "Link in first comment"

## TONE DIFFERENTIATION

Facebook content uses the same substance as LinkedIn but delivers it differently. This is the key creative challenge.

| Dimension | LinkedIn | Facebook |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Opening | "Here's what I've observed across 50 client engagements." | "Can we talk about something I keep seeing?" |
| Authority style | Expert presenting findings | Trusted friend sharing what they've learned |
| Vulnerability | Measured — strategic transparency | Genuine — "I'm still learning this too" |
| Sentence structure | Polished, rhythmic, crafted | More natural, conversational, sometimes messy on purpose |
| Closing | Thought-provoking statement | Invitation to join the conversation |
| Energy | Confident, authoritative | Warm, curious, community-minded |

**The test:** Read the Facebook post out loud. Does it sound like something you'd say to a trusted colleague over coffee? If it sounds like a keynote speech, rewrite it.

## HOOK PATTERNS FOR FACEBOOK

| Pattern | Example | Why it works on Facebook |
|---------|---------|------------------------|
| **Community opener** | "Can we talk about something?" | Creates intimacy, signals conversation |
| **Shared experience** | "If you've ever felt like your best work goes unnoticed..." | Relatability — reader feels seen |
| **Honest question** | "Am I the only one who struggles with this?" | Vulnerability invites responses |
| **Observation** | "I've noticed something about the people who grow fastest." | Insider knowledge, curiosity |
| **Confession** | "I need to be honest about something." | Trust-building, attention-grabbing |
| **Helping frame** | "Something I wish someone had told me earlier:" | Generosity — positions the sharer as helpful |
| **Hot take** | "Unpopular opinion: most content strategies are backwards." | Debate-starter, high comment potential |
| **Story opening** | "Last week something happened that made me rethink everything." | Narrative tension |

## DISCUSSION PROMPTS (Thought Posts)

End every Thought Post with a genuine question that invites specific, personal responses.

**Strong prompts by post type:**

| Post theme | Prompt | Why it works |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| Challenge/pain point | "What's been your biggest struggle with this?" | Invites vulnerability, builds community |
| Success/transformation | "Have you experienced a moment like this? What shifted?" | People love sharing wins |
| Framework/method | "Which of these resonates most with where you are right now?" | Specific, easy to answer |
| Contrarian take | "I'd love to hear the counterargument. What am I missing?" | Respects the audience, invites debate |
| Big picture/trend | "Where do you see this going? I'm genuinely curious." | Intellectual engagement |
| Personal reflection | "Anyone else feel this way?" | Community validation |
