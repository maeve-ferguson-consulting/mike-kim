# Intelligence Gathering Protocol

Phase 0 of content planning. Pull intelligence before the interview to start from strategic awareness, not cold recall.

## When to Run

- **Always** if Slack or other MCP integrations are available
- **Skip** if no integrations are available — ask the client for a verbal intelligence download instead

## With Integrations

### Step 1: Pull Intelligence

Read the last 30 days from relevant Slack channels, CRM, or other connected sources. Look for:
- **Industry intelligence:** Headlines, market shifts, competitor moves, technology changes, regulatory updates
- **Client activity:** Wins, losses, call highlights, event appearances, partnership updates
- **Audience signals:** What got engagement, what questions keep coming up, what topics are trending in the client's space

### Step 2: Separate the Sources

Organize what you find:
- **Market signals** — external forces and trends (maps to Positioning Framework "Why Now")
- **Audience behavior** — what the ideal audience is doing, saying, or missing (maps to "Why This")
- **Client proof** — what the client has done, built, or demonstrated (maps to "Why You")

### Step 3: Synthesize Content Threads

Thread the intelligence through the client's Positioning Framework (if it exists) to identify 2-4 Content Threads. Each thread connects three layers:

**Layer 1: The World (Why Now)**
A market signal that proves one of the framework forces is real and accelerating.

**Layer 2: The Audience (Why This)**
What the ideal audience is doing, missing, or getting wrong in response to that signal.

**Layer 3: The Client (Why You)**
What the client has built, proved, or lived that connects to this thread. This layer gets filled in during the interview.

### Step 4: Present and Transition

Present 2-4 threads as interview openers. Keep them tight — no raw data dumps.

## Without Integrations

Ask the client:
- "What's been happening in your industry the last month that caught your attention?"
- "Any client wins, conversations, or moments that stuck with you?"
- "What's your audience talking about or struggling with right now?"

Use their answers to form loose threads for the calendar build.
