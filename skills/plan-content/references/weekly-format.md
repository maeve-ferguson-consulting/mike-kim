# 8-Field Weekly Format

Each week of the content calendar uses this 8-field format. Every field has a specific purpose in the content pipeline — the Newsletter Writer consumes these fields directly via the Newsletter Brief.

---

## The 8 Fields

### 1. Beacon Topic
The core message or theme in one sharp sentence. Not a title — the idea. This becomes the backbone of the newsletter.

**Good:** "The thing your audience thinks is protecting them is actually the thing making them vulnerable."
**Bad:** "Industry Trends This Week"

### 2. Framework Tether
Which specific elements from the client's Positioning Framework this week connects to. Name them exactly as they appear in the framework.

**Good:** "Why Now: Force 2 (The AI Saviour Trap) + Why This: The Architecture Gap"
**Bad:** "Connects to the client's positioning"

If no Positioning Framework exists, describe the strategic angle: "Contrarian take on [topic]" or "Proof piece showing [transformation]."

### 3. Blind Spot
What the audience can't see that the client can. This is the reveal in the Beacon pattern — the moment the reader's perspective shifts.

**Good:** "They think building a custom GPT protects their IP. It actually makes it instantly replicable."
**Bad:** "The audience doesn't understand the full picture."

### 4. Expanded Vision
What becomes possible when they see what the client sees. Paint it specifically, not generically. This drives the aspirational section of the newsletter.

**Good:** "When they stop chasing leads and start capturing behavioral data, their business becomes an appreciating asset — not just a revenue stream."
**Bad:** "Things will be better when they understand."

### 5. Story Entry Point
The specific story, moment, or observation from intelligence gathering or the interview that carries this week's message. Content enters through narrative, not lecture.

Must trace back to something real — from intelligence or the interview. Never invented.

**Good:** "The conversation with the client who realized her course was being copied by AI — her exact words: 'I've been giving it away for free.'"
**Bad:** "A story about a client who struggled."

### 6. Proof Point
Evidence that grounds the message: client result, data point, market headline, personal experience. Must be real and traceable.

**Good:** "Client X went from 200 leads/month to 200 data profiles/month — same traffic, 40x the asset value."
**Bad:** "Results show this approach works."

### 7. Offer Connection
How this week's content naturally seeds the weekly CTA offer. Name the offer and describe the bridge from the content to the invitation.

**Good:** "This piece establishes why most leaders get the transition wrong (the Blind Spot). The natural next step is the free Leadership Diagnostic — 'find out which trap you're in.' Direct CTA at the close."
**Bad:** "Link to the offer."

### 8. Belief Shift
One belief shift from the Belief Shift Bank (reference by number if the bank exists) that matches this week's framework element. The wrong belief seeds the Blind Spot. The right belief seeds the Expanded Vision.

**Good:** "Belief Shift #8 — They believe building a custom GPT protects their IP → They need to believe it makes their IP instantly replicable."
**Bad:** "Shift their thinking."

If no Belief Shift Bank exists, write the wrong → right belief pair directly.

---

## Rules

- No belief shift reused within a single month
- Each week should be a different framework element than the previous week
- Every story and proof point must trace to the interview or intelligence (nothing fabricated)
- The Blind Spot and Expanded Vision should be specific enough to write from — the Newsletter Writer uses these directly
