# Newsletter Brief Format

The Newsletter Brief is the bridge between the Content Planner and the Newsletter Writer. It's a structured block containing everything the writer needs to start from the strategy, not from scratch.

## The Brief

```
--- NEWSLETTER BRIEF ---
Week: [N] — [Date Range]
Framework Step: [Step name from Positioning Framework]
Framework Element: [Specific element within the step]
CTA Offer: [Offer name]
Belief Shift: #[Number from Belief Shift Bank]
Content Type: Foundational / Momentum

Beacon Topic: [Core message in one sentence]
Blind Spot: [What the audience can't see — seeds the Hook and Reveal]
Expanded Vision: [What becomes possible — seeds the Expansion]
Story Entry Point: [Specific story/moment to enter through]
Proof Point: [Evidence to ground the message]
Offer Connection: [How content bridges to the CTA]
Belief Shift Detail:
  Wrong: [What they currently believe]
  Right: [What they need to believe]

Source Material Needed: [What the client should bring to the writing session]
--- END BRIEF ---
```

## How the Newsletter Writer Uses This

When a brief is pasted into the Newsletter Writer:

1. **The strategic skeleton is set.** The writer does NOT re-ask framework tethering questions or re-select a belief shift.
2. **The writer focuses on bringing it to life** — gathering the client's weekly raw material (call highlights, client moments, life events) and weaving it into the strategic architecture.
3. **The brief provides the architecture. The interview adds the flesh, the stories, the life.**

The writer confirms the brief in one sentence, then asks for raw weekly material:

> "Got it. This week is [Step]: [Element], with CTA to [Offer] and Belief Shift #[N]. The Beacon topic is: [topic]. I have the full brief. Now tell me about your week so I can bring this to life."

## Rules

- One brief per week in the content calendar
- Brief must be self-contained — the writer should be able to work from it without reading the full calendar
- All 8 fields from the weekly format must be present
- Belief Shift number references the Belief Shift Bank (if it exists)
- Brief format must be consistent so the writer can detect it by presence of key fields: "Beacon Topic:", "Framework Step:", "Belief Shift:"
